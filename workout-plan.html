5 HEALTH-DRIVEN MODIFICATIONS — V2.0 → V3.0
1
Compound Strength Sets Reduced
5×3–5 at 90%+ effort creates extreme Valsalva pressure — acutely dangerous with BP in the 130s. Hypertrophy ranges at moderate load are safer and still build muscle effectively.
Was: 5×3–5 @90%+ · Days 1, 2 Now: 4×6–8 @75–80% · Days 1, 2
2
Deadlift Intensity Pulled Back
Deadlifts produce the highest acute blood pressure spike of any lift. Still leading Day 5 on a fresh CNS — just at 70% 1RM, not max effort. Returns to full intensity at Day 90 if BP normalises.
Was: 4×3–5 max effort Now: 3×5 @70% 1RM
3
Day 3 — Intervals → Zone 2
Zone 2 (60–70% max HR, conversational pace) is the most evidence-backed intervention for LDL reduction, visceral fat loss, and BP normalisation. Intervals return at Day 90 once markers improve.
Was: 6–8 round intervals, RPE 8 Now: 35–45 min Zone 2 walk/cycle
4
Day 6 — Upgraded to Zone 2
Was light optional cardio. Now a dedicated Zone 2 session — giving you 4x Zone 2 per week which is the clinical threshold for meaningful LDL and BP impact. Optional pump work still follows after.
Was: 25–35 min incline walk (optional) Now: 35–40 min Zone 2 (mandatory)
5
Post-Dinner Walk Added Daily
10–15 min walk after your largest meal dramatically reduces postprandial triglyceride and glucose spikes — directly impacting LDL particle profile and ALT. Not a workout, just a walk. Every day.
Was: Not in plan Now: 10–15 min after dinner, daily
📋 DAY 90 RETEST PROTOCOL At Day 90 get bloods done: LDL, HDL, triglycerides, ALT, fasting glucose. Also measure BP at home weekly. If LDL drops below 150 and BP normalises below 120 systolic → resume full v2.0 strength protocol (5×3–5, max deadlifts, intervals). If LDL stays above 160 despite full adherence → discuss statins with your GP. That's not a failure — it's genetics.
✅ UNCHANGEDAll accessory work, posterior chain, hypertrophy volume, deload structure, and exercise selection stays exactly as v2.0. Muscle mass is your biggest metabolic ally right now.
