Why The Program Needs Tweaking
🫀 Your Cardiovascular Load Is Too High Right Now
With BP in the 130s and very high LDL, your current 5×3–5 heavy loading on the main lifts creates significant intra-abdominal pressure and acute BP spikes. This isn't a reason to stop lifting — it's a reason to restructure temporarily until your numbers improve.
Workout Tweaks (Apply Now)
🔻
Reduce Max Strength Sets Temporarily
5×3–5 on squat, deadlift, bench creates extreme Valsalva pressure — dangerous with BP 130s. Drop to controlled hypertrophy rep ranges until BP normalises.
Was: 5×3–5 @90%+ effort Now: 4×6–8 @75–80% effort
➕
Add Zone 2 Cardio — 4x/Week
Zone 2 (conversational pace, ~60–70% max HR) is the single most evidence-backed intervention for LDL reduction, visceral fat loss, and BP normalisation. Replace Day 3 intervals with Zone 2 for 90 days.
Was: Intervals Day 3 only Now: 35–45 min Zone 2, 4x/week
🚶
Post-Meal Walks — Every Day
10–15 min walk after your largest meal dramatically reduces postprandial glucose and triglyceride spikes, which directly impacts LDL particle size and insulin sensitivity. Non-negotiable for your profile.
Add: 10–15 min walk after dinner daily
⚠️
No Breath-Holding on Heavy Lifts
Avoid full Valsalva maneuver on heavy sets. Use controlled breathing — exhale on exertion. Reduces acute BP spikes significantly.
Technique: Exhale on concentric, inhale on eccentric
🏃
Deadlift — Reduce to 3×5 with Moderate Load
Deadlifts create the highest systemic BP elevation of any lift. Keep them — they're great for overall metabolic health — but reduce to moderate intensity for 90 days.
Was: 4×3–5 max effort Now: 3×5 @70% 1RM, controlled
💪
Keep All Hypertrophy Work
Muscle mass is your biggest metabolic ally — it improves insulin sensitivity and LDL clearance. All accessory work stays. Volume-based training at moderate intensity is ideal for your metabolic goals.
Keep: All accessory + isolation work unchanged
